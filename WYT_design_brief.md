# Walk Your Talk — Design System Brief (v2)

**Purpose:** reference document for Claude Design to produce on-brand visual work (training decks, one-pagers, case studies, LinkedIn visuals, landing pages) for Walk Your Talk.

**Brand in one line:** team- and leadership programs for mission-driven organizations, combining performance with human-centered leadership. Three thematic pillars: *Leading by listening*, *Strength through vulnerability*, *Speeding up by slowing down*.

**Languages:** bilingual — Dutch primary, English equivalent. All archetypes must work at realistic line lengths in both languages.

---

## 1. Color palette

Extracted from the Walk Your Talk logo (canonical source), cross-checked against PPTX theme and title slide.

| Role | Hex | Name | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Primary** | `#0D654A` | Deep forest | Headings, logo primary ring, section titles, icon fills, key stat numbers. Dominant color — 30–40% of visual weight. |
| **Secondary** | `#59A991` | Sage teal | Logo secondary ring, diagram mid-tier, subtle section accents, agenda panel tint. |
| **Accent** | `#E19C55` | Warm amber | Current-section markers, active-state arrows (`➤`), highlight callouts, logo amber ring. Sparse use — reserve for things that should actually draw the eye. Target 5–10% of visual weight. |
| **Cream** | `#FDEAC6` | Pale cream | Supporting tier in diagrams, soft icon backgrounds, bridging color between green and amber. |
| **Sand** | `#D6C9AF` | Earth sand | Title slides, section-break slides, closing slides, quote cards, reflective visuals. *This is WYT's "slow" color — use for moments of pause.* |
| **Mint tint** | `#E6F3EA` | Mist mint | Default content-slide background. Soft, warm, low-contrast with text. |
| **Stone gray** | `#BFBFBF` | — | Lowest tier / tertiary info, axis lines, dividers. Never for primary content. |
| **Ink** | `#1A1A1A` | Near-black | Body text on light backgrounds. Avoid pure `#000000`. |
| **Base light** | `#FFFFFF` | White | Content areas, icon fills on dark. |

**Palette rules:**
- Deep forest dominates; amber is the sharp accent (roughly 70/10 ratio).
- Sage and cream are *supporting* — never equal weight to forest or amber.
- Sand is the "slowness" color — title slides, reflective quote cards, closing slides, LinkedIn-style visuals. *Avoid sand on content-dense slides; it hurts readability.*
- Gray is structural (lines, tertiary labels) — never decorative.
- No blue. No navy. Ever.

## 2. Typography

**Two-font system** matching WYT's brand duality (performance × humanity):

### Primary — Montserrat (sans-serif)
Default for all structural content: headings, body, UI, training decks, one-pagers, diagrams, labels. Modern geometric sans-serif, matches `walkyourtalk.training`.

### Secondary — Playfair Display (serif)
Reserved for *reflective* content: quote cards, LinkedIn visuals on introspective themes, section break titles, book-like one-pagers. Use when the content is slow, personal, humanistic — not instructional.

> **Rule of thumb:** if the content is a *how*, use Montserrat. If it's a *why* or *what if*, consider Playfair.

### Hierarchy — slides (16:9, 1920×1080)

| Element | Font | Size | Weight | Case | Color |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Title slide hero | Montserrat | 54pt | Bold | Sentence | White on dome, `#0D654A` on light |
| Slide title | Montserrat | 40pt | Bold | Sentence | `#0D654A` |
| Section header | Montserrat | 24pt | Bold | Sentence | `#0D654A` |
| Body | Montserrat | 16pt | Regular | Sentence | `#1A1A1A` |
| Supporting body | Montserrat | 14pt | Regular | Sentence | `#1A1A1A` |
| Caption / attribution | Montserrat | 11pt | Italic | Sentence | `#1A1A1A` at 70% |
| Emphasis in body | Montserrat | — | SemiBold | — | `#0D654A` |
| Quote slides (reflective) | Playfair Display | 36pt | Regular | Sentence | `#0D654A` |

### Hierarchy — documents / one-pagers

|---|---|---|---|---|

### Typography rules
- Left-align body always. Center-align only titles on title slides and LinkedIn visuals.
- Line spacing: 1.3× for body, 1.1× for titles, 1.2× for quotes.
- No ALL CAPS except LinkedIn visual titles and micro-labels (≤3 words) in amber.
- Italic is for captions, attributions, and pull quotes — not for emphasis (use weight).
- Never more than two fonts in one deliverable.

## 3. Layout principles

**Grid:** 12-column mental model, generous gutters. Slides at 1920×1080 with 96px outer margin.

**Whitespace:** every major element needs ≥40px breathing room. Fill 60–70% of the canvas at most — negative space is a brand signal (*speeding up by slowing down*).

**Rhythm:** consistent vertical spacing — 48px between sections, 24px within a section, 12px within a paragraph. No mixed spacing values.

**Composition:** favor **asymmetric two-column** layouts (40/60 or 35/65) over symmetric splits. One side holds content, the other an image, diagram, or whitespace.

**Alignment:** left-align body and lists. Center only titles on title slides and closing slides.

---

## 4. Visual tone

**Is:** warm, organic, grounded, human, spacious. Earthy but not rustic. Modern but not corporate-slick. Serene but confident.

**Is not:** tech-SaaS blue, AI-gradient, Silicon Valley minimal, corporate navy, "wellness" decorative (no lotus flowers, no hand-lettered script, no purple sunrise gradients), stock-photo handshakes, 3D illustrated figures.

**Inspiration anchors:** School for Moral Ambition, Patagonia reports, The Correspondent long-form. Earth-toned, text-confident, photography-supported. Not: typical leadership-training decks with blue gradients.

**Register:** warmer and more personal than the current `walkyourtalk.training` homepage. Closer to Robbert-Jan's LinkedIn voice — reflective, direct, philosophical — than to "high performance consulting" tone.

---

## 5. Imagery direction

- **Landscapes** — mountains, water, open horizon, fields at golden hour. Signals space, perspective, slowness.
- **People in natural settings** — small groups in conversation outdoors, individuals in reflective poses, always in natural light, never in office settings. Warm backlight preferred.
- **Atmospheric mood** — misty, soft, sunset, dawn. Not bright daylight, not fluorescent.
- **Avoid** — stock handshakes, whiteboard shots, skyscraper meetings, generic "team success" imagery.
- **Reference images** — the four LinkedIn visuals uploaded by Robbert-Jan (reflection on rock, man walking into sunrise, group conversing at sunset) set the tone.

## 6. Recurring motifs (signature elements)

Use at least one per deliverable to maintain family resemblance:

1. **Circle / arc / dome** — overlapping circles from the logo, dome rising on title slides, curved image frames, circular icon backgrounds. Circles are the brand's shape language — treat as a signature.
2. **Landscape imagery** — see §5.
3. **Orange active-state arrow** (`➤`) — amber, marks current section in agendas and process steps. Signature wayfinding.
4. **Filled forest-green circles with white icons** — row-start markers, section icons, step numbers.

## 7. Slide archetypes (canonical layouts)

Keep these consistent across decks so participants recognize the structure. Framework visuals (pyramid, quadrants, ladder) can vary *within their archetype*: the Communication Pyramid may appear as pyramid, staircase, or table depending on the metaphor used that day. Other elements (colors, fonts, spacing) stay fixed.

### A. Title slide
- Full-bleed landscape photo (mountain/water/field at golden hour). Sand `#D6C9AF` overlay at 30% if needed for contrast.
- Deep forest half-dome rising from bottom center, anchoring the title.
- Title in white Montserrat Bold 54pt, centered inside dome.
- Subtitle ("Clear is Kind"–style) in white Montserrat Bold 20pt.
- Client name in white Montserrat Italic 14pt, lower third.
- Logo bottom-right.

### B. Agenda
- 40/60 asymmetric split. Left: mint `#E6F3EA` panel with 4–6 items, left-aligned Montserrat 18pt. Right: image with curved-left-edge frame.
- Active item prefixed with amber `➤` and set in SemiBold.
- Logo top-left corner, small.

### C. Section divider (replaces current slide 6)
- Full canvas in sand `#D6C9AF` OR deep forest `#0D654A`.
- Large section number (60pt Montserrat Light, amber) + section title (40pt Montserrat Bold, forest on sand / white on forest).
- Optional landscape image at 35% opacity in background.
- Never pure-text.

### D. Concept with 2×2 matrix
- 4 cards on a 2×2 grid. Each card has a colored top-bar (forest / amber / sage / cream) and a short label.
- Axis labels outside grid in Montserrat Italic 12pt stone gray.
- Title top-left, whitespace around grid.

### E. Concept with diagram (circle / pyramid / flow)
- Diagram occupies right 60%. Left 40%: bulleted explanation or pull quote.
- Diagram tiers use palette colors *with meaning* — top: forest, mid: amber, lower: sage or cream, base: gray. Never arbitrary.
- Framework can swap metaphors (pyramid → staircase → table) while keeping tier colors consistent.

### F. Concept with vertical process (5–7 steps) — fixes slide 13
- Left column: label chips. Alternate **forest** and **amber** row-by-row (not rainbow gradient).
- Right column: one-sentence explanation per step in Montserrat 14pt.
- Consistent chip width, 48px row spacing.
- If one step needs extra emphasis, use amber; otherwise forest. Stone gray for completed/deprecated steps only.

### G. Quote / reflection slide
- Playfair Display 36pt quote in deep forest, left-aligned.
- Attribution below in Montserrat Italic 14pt.
- Single thin amber divider line between quote and attribution.
- Generous whitespace. Sand background optional for stronger pause-feel.

### H. Closing slide
- Sand `#D6C9AF` or mint `#E6F3EA` background.
- Logo large, centered.
- Contact info + QR code below in Montserrat 14pt.
- Short line: "Dank je." / "Thank you." or tagline.

### I. LinkedIn visual (1080×1350 portrait) — new archetype
- Full-bleed photograph (landscape + optional people, golden hour palette).
- Top 25–30% reserved for text.
- Title ALL CAPS, centered, 2-line max. **Playfair Display Regular** for reflective themes, **Montserrat Bold** for assertive themes.
- Subtitle in Montserrat Regular, one sentence, max two lines.
- Walk Your Talk wordmark + logo mark bottom center.
- No amber accents — trust the photography.

## 8. Don'ts

- **No mixed primary fonts within a deliverable.** One primary (Montserrat). Playfair only for specific reflective moments.
- **No arbitrary color in diagrams.** Every color must mean something (tier, status, category). Rainbow gradients = refactor needed.
- **No 3D stock imagery** (like the question-mark figure currently on slide 7). Flat iconography or real photography only.
- **No decorative accent lines under titles.** Whitespace separates.
- **No pure text section-divider slides.** Always one visual anchor (color field, image, oversized number).
- **No blue.** No navy. No tech-SaaS gradients.
- **No cream/beige defaults** (`#F5F5DC`, `#FAF0E6`). Use defined sand `#D6C9AF` or cream `#FDEAC6`.
- **No body text under 14pt on slides.** Back-of-the-room legibility matters.
- **No HIGH PERFORMANCE language** in titles or section names unless client-specified. Prefer verbs of impact: *grow, build, listen, deepen*.
